# Tabylga 3-Minute Pitch Script

## 0:00-0:30 Hook

"$1.1 billion entered Kyrgyzstan tourism in 2025. A third disappeared into the grey cash economy."

Kyrgyzstan is already a major tourism destination, with 8.8 million visits and fast-growing demand for authentic mountain, nomadic, and eco-tourism experiences. But the money trail is fragmented. Tourists pay in cash, small merchants stay invisible, and the Ministry cannot see revenue flow in real time.

Tabylga turns that missing visibility into a national tourism data layer.

## 0:30-1:00 Problem

Tourism in Kyrgyzstan is not one broken app. It is a broken operating system.

A tourist books a yurt camp through one channel, pays a driver in cash, buys food from a family cafe, enters a park with another payment flow, and leaves no unified transaction trail. Micro-merchants are excluded from digital infrastructure because onboarding is too complex, too expensive, or not designed for rural tourism.

The result is three gaps: fragmented tourist journeys, payment leakage, and no trusted live dashboard for public-sector tourism planning.

## 1:00-2:30 Live Demo

First, the tourist opens Tabylga and chooses a route: Issyk-Kul, Naryn, Osh, or a custom multi-day trip. The app shows verified merchants, rest points, attractions, transport, and activities in one flow.

Next, the tourist selects a merchant, pays through a QR or card flow, and receives a clean digital receipt. The merchant does not need a heavy POS system. A phone and a verified profile are enough.

Now we switch to the B2G dashboard. Every transaction updates tourism revenue analytics: region, category, merchant performance, tourist flow, and spending trend. The Ministry can see where revenue is moving today, which regions are underperforming, and which categories are growing.

This is the core Tabylga loop: discover, pay, verify, analyze. Tourists get a better journey, merchants get digital demand, and the government gets live economic visibility without forcing small businesses into complex systems.

## 2:30-3:30 Business Model

Tabylga has four revenue streams.

First, transaction fees. We take a small platform fee on verified tourism payments. At scale, even a 1% fee on a fraction of the $1.1 billion tourism market creates a meaningful revenue base.

Second, merchant subscriptions. Guesthouses, tour operators, cafes, transport providers, and activity vendors can pay for enhanced visibility, analytics, and promotional placement.

Third, B2G analytics. The Ministry of Tourism and regional administrations can subscribe to dashboards, reporting, heatmaps, and planning tools that show tourism revenue and infrastructure demand.

Fourth, ecosystem partnerships. Banks, payment providers, insurance companies, telecoms, and travel brands can integrate into Tabylga routes and merchant flows.

This gives us a blended model: payments for volume, subscriptions for predictability, and government analytics for strategic value.

## 3:30-4:00 Team + Ask

Our team understands the local tourism market, the payment gap, and the technical architecture needed to connect tourists, micro-merchants, and public-sector decision makers.

We are asking for support to launch the pilot in priority tourism corridors, onboard the first verified merchants, integrate payment partners, and present the analytics dashboard as Kyrgyzstan's live tourism revenue layer.

Tabylga is not just a travel app. It is digital infrastructure for the next stage of Kyrgyz tourism.
