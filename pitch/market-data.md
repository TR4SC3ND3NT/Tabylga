# Market Data for Slides

## Core Market Size

- Tourism revenue entering Kyrgyzstan in 2025: $1.1 billion.
- Total visits: 8.8 million.
- Tourism businesses: 148,100.
- Visitor mix: 95% from CIS countries.

## Infrastructure Signals

- Rest Points infrastructure creates a national backbone for route-based tourism services.
- KICB ELQR acceptance footprint: 67,000 points.
- QR and card acceptance are expanding, but rural tourism merchants still need lighter onboarding and tourism-specific visibility.

## Problem Signals

- A large share of tourism spending still moves through cash or disconnected payment channels.
- Micro-merchants such as yurt camps, family cafes, drivers, guides, craft sellers, and park-adjacent vendors often stay outside formal digital tourism infrastructure.
- Fragmented payments make it difficult to measure regional tourism revenue, category demand, and merchant performance in real time.

## Slide Framing

- $1.1B market: Kyrgyzstan tourism is already economically significant.
- 8.8M visits: demand is present; the missing layer is conversion and visibility.
- 148,100 businesses: the merchant base is broad, fragmented, and underserved.
- 95% CIS visitors: early go-to-market can optimize for familiar regional payment behavior before expanding globally.
- Rest Points plus ELQR: existing infrastructure can be connected into a unified tourism commerce and analytics layer.

## Tabylga Opportunity

Tabylga turns tourist payments into structured national tourism intelligence. Each verified payment can show where tourists spend, which regions capture value, which categories grow, and which small merchants need support.
